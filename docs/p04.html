Portfolio 4

In this project, I use the Waves 1 and 2 of the National Longitudinal Study of Adolescent to Adult Health (Add Health) to test the following hypothesis:

It is hypothesized that young adolescents’ sexual risk-taking (Motivations to Engage in Risky Behaviors, T1) will be associated with higher levels of substance use (Tobacco, Alcohol, Drugs, T1; total effect). Additionally, this effect will persist after controlling for (Parents’ Attitudes, T1; partial effect). However, the relationship between sexual risk-taking and substance use will be stronger among boys than girls (T1; moderated effect).

The result shows that motivation for risky behaviors was positively associated with substance use. This effect persisted even after accounting for parents’ attitudes. However, the moderation hypothesis was not supported; the data indicated that the relationship between motivation and substance use did not significantly differ between boys and girls.
