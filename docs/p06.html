This is the last portion of the Add Health analysis. In this piece, I will test a third hypothesis:

It is hypothesized that neighborhood environment (Neighborhood, T1) will predict lower levels of adolescent depressive symptoms (Feeling Scale, T2; total effect). However, this effect will be attenuated after support from family and friends (Protective Factors, T1) is controlled for (partial effect).

The neighborhood environment was found to be a significant longitudinal predictor of mental health. A more positive Wave 1 neighborhood environment predicted lower levels of Wave 2 depressive symptoms (β = -0.56, p < .001), as hypothesized. This effect was not attenuated and remained significant after Wave 1 Protective Factors were included in the model.
